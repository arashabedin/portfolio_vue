Privacy Policy — PD3 Læseforståelse

Last updated: 21 April 2026

Controller: Arash Abedin, Denmark

Contact (privacy): arash.abedin@gmail.com

1. Who we are

This privacy policy describes how Arash Abedin (“we”, “us”) processes information when you use the mobile application PD3 Læseforståelse (the “App”).

2. Scope

This policy applies to the App and the page where we host this policy: https://arash.dk/pd3-laeseforstaaelse/privacy-policy.html. It is intended to meet common transparency expectations under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and good practice for app distribution (e.g. Google Play).

3. Summary

The App is designed to help you practise PD3-style reading comprehension. We do not require you to create an account. Based on the current version of the App, your answers and progress are stored locally on your device and are not transmitted to our servers by the App itself.

If that changes in a future version (for example cloud sync, analytics, or support tools), we will update this policy and, where required, ask for your consent or provide other lawful bases.

4. Information the App processes

4.1 Data stored on your device (local storage)

The App may store information such as:

  • Which exams you have started or completed, and related status flags.
  • Your answers, timers, navigation state, and similar session data needed to run a practice session and show results.

This data stays on your device unless you copy it elsewhere yourself (for example screenshots or device backups—see below).

4.2 Data we do not intentionally collect through the App

The current App version does not:

  • Ask for your name, email, or other direct identifiers for using the App.
  • Use analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs as part of the application code we ship.
  • Access your precise location, camera, microphone, or contacts for App features.

4.3 Device and system information

Your operating system and app store (e.g. Google Play on Android) may process technical information according to their own policies (for example app updates, security, and aggregated statistics). We do not control that processing.

4.4 Internet access

The App may use network connectivity provided by the platform (for example to load packaged web content in the WebView or for system services). We do not operate a backend API for core App features in the version reviewed for this policy.

5. Purposes and legal bases (GDPR)

We process the local information described above to provide the App’s core functionality (running practice sessions, saving progress on your device, showing results).

Legal basis: Article 6(1)(b) GDPR — processing necessary to perform the service you request by using the App; and where applicable Article 6(1)(f) GDPR — legitimate interests in maintaining a functional, reliable practice experience on your device (for example storing session state).

We do not use this information for behavioural advertising in the current App version.

6. Sharing and disclosure

We do not sell your personal data. We do not transmit your practice answers or progress to our servers in the current App version.

Information may be processed by infrastructure providers only in the ordinary course of providing the App through app stores and device platforms (for example Google for Android distribution), under their terms and privacy policies.

7. International transfers

If we later use services that process data outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we will describe that here and rely on appropriate safeguards (for example Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions) as required by GDPR.

8. Retention

Data stored locally remains until you clear app data, uninstall the App, or clear site data for the App in your device settings, depending on your platform. We do not retain copies on our systems for the processing described in section 4.1, because we do not receive that data.

9. Security

We design the App to keep practice data on your device. You should protect your device with a passcode or biometric lock if you are concerned about others accessing your device.

10. Your rights (EEA/UK)

Depending on your situation, you may have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing, object, and data portability, and to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. For purely local processing that never leaves your device, some rights are exercised directly on your device (for example by clearing storage).

To exercise rights against us as controller, contact arash.abedin@gmail.com. You may also lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority; in Denmark this is the Danish Data Protection Agency (Datatilsynet) — https://www.datatilsynet.dk/.

11. Children

The App is not intended for children under 16. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child has used the App in a way that concerns you, contact us at the email above.

12. Changes

We may update this policy when we change the App or legal requirements. We will post the new version at https://arash.dk/pd3-laeseforstaaelse/privacy-policy.html and update the “Last updated” date.
